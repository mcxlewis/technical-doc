What is a Nuzlocke?

Nuzlocke is a way of playing a Pokémon game that is extremely challenging. There are many formats, but there are three basic rules you must follow.

  1. You are only allowed to catch the first Pokémon you encounter per area. If it faints or flees, you lose your chance to catch any Pokémon in that area.
  2. If your Pokémon faints, it dies. It must be released or stored in the PC permanently.
  3. Nickname all of your Pokémon. This is not necessary for completing the challenge, but is part of the fun.
Your goal is to beat all eight gyms and the Elite Four and become Champion without whiting out. If you white out at any point in the game, your game ends and you must start over.

Rules

There are some common optional rules as well:

  • Duplicate Rule: A player may try again if they have already caught the species that appears. You may set yourself a limit of attempts to keep it a challenge.
  • Shiny Clause: If a player encounters a Shiny, they may try to capture it even if they have already caught a Pokémon in that area. This does not count as an encounter.
    1:4096 chance
  • Random Starter: Your starter is chosen based on the last digit of your trainer ID:
    1-3: Grass | 4-6: Water | 7-9: Fire | 0: Player may choose
Clauses

There are some clauses that non-hardcore players apply to make the beginning of the game smoother or for technical gameplay purposes.

  • Diversity Clause: At the start of the game, a player may be allowed to try to catch more than one species of Pokémon in an area as to try to build up a team.
  • Gift Clause: Allows acceptance of in-game trades and given Pokémon, and that a player may be allowed to catch a Pokémon for purposes of an in-game trade.

Use these clauses to your discretion. They are not excuses to cheat.

Other Versions

There are dozens of other types of Nuzlockes that follow more rules than just the mandatory ones to make the experience more unique and/or challenging. Here are a few examples:

  • Wedlocke

    Pokémon are paired up by gender and can only be swapped out with each other during battle until they are both KO'd.

  • Egglocke

    Randomly bred and hatched eggs are the only ones able to be used, whether they are bred by the player or sent to them.

  • Hardcorelocke

    Items and overlevelling (leveling your team higher than a gym teacher's ace) are not allowed.

  • Giftlocke

    A player can only use their starter and gift Pokémon obtained without catching.

These all seemed far too daunting to me and I chose to simply follow the mandatory rules with the Diversity Clause and Gift Clause.
